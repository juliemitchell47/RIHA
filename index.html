Letter From The Governor

Rhode Island has been on a comeback for several years. Since pulling out of the recession, Rhode Island's economy and housing market have been experiencing strong positive trends. I am proud to say that part of our economic comeback is a result of the work of RIHousing.

RIHousing has helped generations of Rhode Islanders across the state find a place to call 'home'. From those looking for affordable rental options, to first-time homebuyers looking to put down roots in our state, to homeowners struggling to maintain their homes, and developers and community partners needing financing to support the creation of new homes and neighborhoods. RIHousing's work touches each of us.

2018 was a strong year for the corporation. RIHousing pumped $780 million into the state's economy, providing homes for Rhode Islanders that are within their financial reach while creating jobs, building strong communities and promoting economic development. These housing investments strengthen our workforce, boost our construction and real estate industries, and bring us closer to our goal of ensuring every Rhode Island family has a home within their financial reach.

But, RIHousing does not operate in a vacuum. With the support of elected officials, state and municipal offices, the business community and others, the agency works with an array of partners all committed to the same goal of revitalizing the state’s economy and supporting its residents. For four years my administration has fought hard to make Rhode Island a place where people have confidence that they can make a good life for themselves and their families. A safe home is critical to reaching that goal.

As I shared in my inaugural address earlier this year, let's commit ourselves to sustaining the comeback until every Rhode Islander is included, and to making choices that will position our state for success for the long haul. Part of that success is ensuring housing is affordable to Rhode Island’s residents. That families have a home where their children can grow, seniors can age in place, young people can become first-time homeowners, and that we can attract businesses here and retain top talent.

These may seem like lofty goals, but we have the energy and commitment to make them happen. Together we can achieve great things for our state. As RIHousing begins a new chapter in its history, let’s continue to work together and support them in these efforts.
